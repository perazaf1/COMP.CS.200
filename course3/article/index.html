Review: Common Web Design Mistakes to Avoid (wparena.com)

A review of the article about errors in web websites design and how to avoid them.

Summary of the Article

The article "Common Web Design Mistakes to Avoid" on wparena.com outlines several frequently encountered errors that can negatively impact a website's user experience, accessibility, and overall effectiveness. These mistakes range from issues with navigation and usability to problems with design elements and content presentation. Key points highlighted include avoiding clutter, ensuring mobile responsiveness, optimizing for speed, using appropriate typography and color palettes, implementing clear calls to action, and prioritizing accessibility for all users. The article highlights the importance of user-centered design principles and provides practical tips for avoiding these incidents and creating a more engaging and effective website. All the tips are really helpful, for both beginners or intermediate or avdanced software developpers. When we read the article, it might seems logic, but in reality these kind of "rules" we need to follow them when coding and designing our website to avoid the user frustration.

What I Learned

Reading this article really opened my eyes to how even the smallest design choices can significantly impact user experience. I realized that it's crucial to consider mobile users right from the start of a project, rather than treating them as an afterthought. The tips on website speed, especially optimizing images and videos, were a game-changer for me since I often overlook these aspects during the initial design phase. I also learned how poor contrast and unreadable fonts can severely affect accessibility, which reinforced the importance of inclusive design practices. Additionally, the article highlighted the need for clear calls to action that effectively guide users through the website. From the article on common web design mistakes, I picked up a few more valuable lessons. For instance, neglecting mobile responsiveness is a major pitfall. Ensuring that a website is mobile-friendly can greatly enhance user engagement and satisfaction. Another key point was the impact of slow page load times. Optimizing website speed not only improves user experience but also boosts search engine rankings (optimize SEO with useful tools). Lastly, the importance of intuitive navigation and avoiding cluttered designs was highlighted, as these factors can make or break a user's interaction with a site.

Why this article was benefical

This article was particularly meaningful and beneficial because it provided a concise and practical overview of common web design mistakes, specifically aimed at preventing them. Many of the points discussed were things I was not so aware of, but the article clearly articulated the reasons why these practices should be avoided and offered specific solutions. For example, instead of simply saying "optimize for phones," it explained the impact of responsiveness and how much users do uses their mobile phone to go on the internet, so that is why this is so important to look for the compatibility and the responsiveness. The article also served as a valuable checklist to keep in mind when planning and executing future web design projects. It reinforced the importance in my mind of putting the user first and constantly testing and iterating to improve the overall website experience. This is also some tips that I have encountered in my course HTI.100 , which was about human-centered design and all the guidelines that make user not frustrated.
